1. **NAME AND AREA OF OPERATION**

1.1 The Society shall be known as CHAMPION CITY MOBILITY, hereinafter referred to as “the SACCO”.
1.2 The SACCO shall operate within the Republic of Kenya, with initial pilot in Nairobi.
1.3 The SACCO is registered under the Co-operative Societies Act, Cap 490, and shall operate in accordance with the Act, its Rules, and these by-laws.

3. OBJECTIVES OF THE SACCO

The objectives of the SACCO are to:

a) Promote thrift and financial discipline among members by providing a safe and convenient avenue for saving.
b) Provide affordable credit to members for development, emergencies, and income-generating activities.
c) Offer members digital access to savings, loans, financial education, and credit scoring.
d) Promote the welfare and economic growth of boda-boda operators through motorbike-based financing, safety incentives, and risk-mitigation programs.
e) Establish partnerships with insurers, garages, spare-part vendors, and training institutions to support the boda-boda sector.
f) Engage in any other socio-economic activities approved by the AGM and permitted by law.


---

4. CO-OPERATIVE PRINCIPLES AND VALUES

The SACCO shall uphold co-operative principles including:

Voluntary and open membership

Democratic member control

Economic participation by members

Autonomy and independence

Education, training and information

Co-operation among co-operatives

Concern for community


Core values include: Integrity, Transparency, Accountability, Equity, Innovation and Professionalism.

5. MEMBERSHIP

5.1 Common Bond

Membership shall be open to:

Licensed boda-boda operators in Kenya

Persons completing digital registration and motorbike verification

Individuals who pay the required entrance fee and minimum share capital

Traders or service providers within the boda-boda ecosystem (subject to AGM approval)


5.2 Admission Procedure

An applicant shall:

a) Complete digital or physical registration
b) Pay KSh 500 non-refundable entrance fee
c) Purchase a minimum of 50 shares (KSh 5,000)
d) Provide a copy of ID, KRA PIN, and motorbike details (where applicable)
e) Be approved by the Management Committee

5.3 Rights of Members

Members shall have the right to:

a) Attend and vote in general meetings (one member, one vote)
b) Receive dividends and interest on savings
c) Access SACCO loans and services subject to policy
d) Elect and be elected to committees
e) Request information in accordance with the law
f) Withdraw from membership subject to by-laws

5.4 Obligations of Members

Members shall:

a) Uphold these by-laws and resolutions of meetings
b) Participate in SACCO activities and education programmes
c) Repay loans as agreed
d) Promote the SACCO’s good name and business

5.5 Termination of Membership

Membership ceases through:

Death

Written withdrawal (60 days’ notice)

Expulsion for misconduct or fraud

Permanent insanity as certified by a medical practitioner

Failure to meet membership obligations


A member under expulsion is entitled to be heard before a final decision is made.


---

6. SHARES AND SAVINGS

6.1 Shares

a) Minimum share capital: 50 shares (KSh 5,000)
b) Shares are non-withdrawable but refundable upon termination minus liabilities.
c) No member shall hold more than 25% of total paid-up shares.

6.2 Savings

a) Savings are withdrawable subject to 60 days’ notice.
b) Members must maintain the minimum share capital at all times.
c) Savings may be held in a digital wallet or SACCO savings account.


---

7. LOANS

7.1 Loan Types & Maximums

a) Emergency Loan

Max: 50% of savings

Tenure: 1 month


b) Normal Loan

Up to 3× (savings + shares)

Eligibility: 6 months active membership

3 guarantors required


c) Logbook Loan (Motorcycle-based)

Up to 70% of motorbike value

2 guarantors required

Motorbike must be insured (comprehensive or TPO + tracking)


d) Title Deed Loan

Up to 50% of land value

3 guarantors

Land must be verified


7.2 Interest Rates (2026)

Emergency: 10% flat

Normal loan: 1.5% p.m. reducing

Logbook: 1.25% p.m. reducing

Title deed: 1.1% p.m. reducing


The AGM may review these rates annually.

7.3 Guarantors

Guarantors are jointly and severally liable.

A member may guarantee a maximum of five loans at a time.

Guarantor substitution requires written approval from all parties.


7.4 Loan Default & Recovery

a) Loans unpaid for 30 days attract 5% monthly penalty.
b) At 90 days, recovery begins from:

1. Borrower’s savings


2. Borrower’s shares


3. Guarantors
c) Defaulters may be listed with CRB, suspended, or taken to small-claims court.




---

8. GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE

8.1 General Meeting

The AGM is the supreme authority of the SACCO.

Every member has one vote, regardless of shares owned.

AGMs shall be convened within four months after the end of the financial year.


8.2 Management Committee (Board)

Composed of 7 members:
Chairman, Vice-Chairman, Secretary, Treasurer, and 3 committee members

Elected for a 3-year term, renewable once

Responsible for governance, policy, approvals, staffing, and budgeting


8.3 Supervisory Committee

3 members elected by the AGM

Responsible for internal audits, risk oversight, and compliance


8.4 Credit Committee

3 members

Reviews and recommends loans to the Board


8.5 Removal from Office

An official may be removed for:

Gross misconduct

Conflict of interest

Failure to attend 3 consecutive meetings without apology

Misappropriation of SACCO funds



---

9. FUNDS, ACCOUNTS & FINANCIAL YEAR

9.1 Sources of Funds

Share capital

Savings

Entrance fees

Investment income

Grants and donations

Surpluses from operations


9.2 Financial Year

The SACCO’s financial year runs from 1st January to 31st December.

9.3 Distribution of Surplus

AGM shall allocate surplus as follows:

At least 70%: Dividends/interest to members

20%: Statutory reserve fund

10%: Education fund, welfare or community projects



---

10. BOOKS, ACCOUNTS & RECORDS

The SACCO shall maintain updated digital and physical records, including:

Members register

Savings and share ledgers

Loan register

Minutes of meetings

Inventory register

Annual audited accounts (certified by an approved auditor)

CRB compliance records

Asset register


Records shall be kept for at least 7 years.


---

11. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

Disputes shall be resolved through:

1. Internal reconciliation


2. Mediation or arbitration under the Co-operative Tribunal


3. Courts of law (as last resort)




---

12. AMENDMENT OF BY-LAWS

These by-laws may be amended by a two-thirds (⅔) majority vote during a General Meeting, and take effect upon approval by the Commissioner for Co-operative Development.


---

13. DISSOLUTION

The SACCO may be dissolved by a three-quarters (¾) vote of members in a Special General Meeting or by an order of the Co-operative Tribunal.
Remaining assets shall be transferred to another SACCO with similar objectives after settlement of liabilities.
